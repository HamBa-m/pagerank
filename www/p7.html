Accelerated Monte Carlo Tree Search for Autonomous Driving: A Deep Reinforcement Learning Approach

Abstract: Autonomous driving is a complex and challenging problem that requires real-time decision making in uncertain and dynamic environments. Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) is a promising reinforcement learning algorithm for planning and decision making in autonomous driving, but its sample efficiency and computational complexity can limit its applicability to real-world scenarios. In this paper, we propose a novel approach to accelerate MCTS for autonomous driving using deep reinforcement learning techniques. We use a deep neural network to approximate the value and policy functions in MCTS, and train the network using a combination of supervised learning and reinforcement learning on a large dataset of driving scenarios. We also introduce several optimization techniques, such as batch normalization and residual connections, to improve the performance and stability of the network. We demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach on several benchmark driving tasks, such as lane following and obstacle avoidance, and show that it achieves significant improvements in both sample efficiency and computational speed compared to standard MCTS methods. Furthermore, we evaluate the robustness and safety of our approach using simulation and real-world experiments, and show that it can handle complex and challenging driving scenarios with high accuracy and reliability. Finally, we discuss open research problems and future directions in applying accelerated MCTS with deep reinforcement learning to autonomous driving, and relate our findings to the broader context of reinforcement learning, as described in "Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction" by Sutton and Barto.
